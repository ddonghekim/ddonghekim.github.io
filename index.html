About

🧑‍💻

My research goal is to make blockchain systems both secure and efficient — across the consensus, network, and application layers — and to extend that trust into the emerging on-chain agent economy.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Korea University, working in the Information Systems Security Lab under the supervision of Prof. Junbeom Hur (issl.korea.ac.kr), with an expected graduation of August 2027. My work spans Bitcoin mixing-service de-anonymization, Ethereum MEV-attack detection, DAG-based MEV reseilient-BFT consensus design, and proof-gated agent wallets — combining large-scale on-chain data analysis with cryptographic and ML modeling.

Research Interests

Application Layer · Forensics

Bitcoin Mixer De-mixing & Forensics

De-anonymizing Bitcoin mixing services by linking deposit and withdrawal transactions — combining on-chain graph analysis, confirmation-time prediction, and ML-based classification to trace funds through privacy-preserving mixers and reveal their anonymity limits.

Application Layer · MEV

Ethereum MEV Attack Detection

Detecting Maximal Extractable Value attacks — frontrunning and transaction-ordering manipulation — on Ethereum DEX and NFT marketplaces, using empirical on-chain analysis and AI-based models to identify attacks in real time.

Consensus Layer

MEV-Resilient Consensus Design

Designing an efficient and secure DAG-based asynchronous BFT consensus algorithm that is resilient to MEV. By enforcing fair transaction ordering and trust-aware routing at the protocol level, it neutralizes ordering-manipulation attacks at their root while improving the latency–throughput trade-off — delivering both security and performance rather than detecting MEV after the fact.

Agentic Systems

Verifiable AI on Ethereum

Proof-gated ERC-4337 agent wallets, randomness-bound inference, and auditable delegation — binding what an autonomous agent computes to what actually executes on-chain, extending blockchain trust to the emerging AI-agent economy.

Research & Projects

Proposal · Ethereum Foundation PhD Fellowship 2026 (Under Review)

Proof-Gated Agent Wallets for the Ethereum Agentic Economy

Formalizes a host-side Grinding Attack on LLM-based agents — structurally equivalent to MEV but invisible to on-chain defenses. Proposes randomness-bound inference enforced by a proof-gated ERC-4337 wallet, plus TLSNotary-based auditable delegation for legacy websites.

2024 – Present · Korea University · Government-funded

AI-based Detection & De-anonymization of Bitcoin Mixing Services

Built large-scale on-chain data pipelines in Python; engineered 22 transaction-level features capturing temporal, structural, and economic properties of mixing behavior. Designed a path-based XGBoost classifier (RFE, 10 optimal features) for mixing-stage classification, plus a C++ de-mixing algorithm using block confirmation timing and dynamic fee policies.

Accuracy 94.95%F1 94.62% ROC-AUC 98.47%De-mix 84.38%

Mixing-Service Algorithm Analysis

Led comparative analysis of Bitcoin and Ethereum mixing services — decentralized vs. centralized structures, zk-SNARKs-based privacy, and anonymity limits. Studied traceback feasibility and mixing vulnerabilities using Chainalysis label data and real transaction data.

  • Advanced to finals of SCAN2024 (Upbit cryptocurrency-tracking forensics competition)

2023 – Present · Korea University

NFT MEV Attacks on Ethereum

First large-scale empirical study of MEV attacks on Ethereum NFT marketplaces. Analyzed ~4M NFT trades over 18 months, detecting 851 real-world frontrunning attacks. Built a ViT+BERT multimodal price model (65.37% accuracy) as the foundation for an autonomous mainnet agent — validated over 17 days. This research directly establishes the methodological basis for real-time MEV detection and motivates structural defenses at the consensus layer via DAG-BFT design.
